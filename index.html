About the Lab

Our laboratory at Boston University focuses on molecular mechanisms of RNA processing in Trypanosoma brucei, a protozoan responsible for diseases like African sleeping sickness and nagana. We explore unique biological processes such as mitochondrial U-insertion/deletion mRNA editing, guide RNA biogenesis, and non-canonical polyadenylation.

Research Description

Our research investigates the molecular mechanisms of RNA processing in Trypanosoma brucei. Key areas include:

  • Mitochondrial U-insertion/deletion mRNA editing and guide-RNA biogenesis
  • Polyadenylation, translation and decay of mitochondrial mRNAs
  • Roles of nuclear non-canonical poly(A) polymerases (ncPAPs) and cytosolic TUTases
  • RNA-decay pathways regulating nuclear gene expression
  • Crystallographic studies for designing trypanocides

We use biochemical, genetic, structural and proteomic approaches in our NIH-funded, state-of-the-art laboratory. Exciting projects are available for graduate and post-doctoral researchers.
